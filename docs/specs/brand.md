## 1. Brand Identity & Symbol

### The Symbol
The brand symbol is a **coffee plant (mata de café)**. The coffee plant represents far more than a simple plant — it is an emblem of dedication, craft, and the culture of specialty coffee.

The coffee plant symbolizes:
- The hard work and dedication of farmers who tend to it with care
- The deep connection between agriculture and a quality product
- A social gathering point — harvesting is a community event that strengthens bonds
- Tradition, effort, and passion for the land and its fruits

The coffee plant is an identity symbol that unites the community around a drink that connects people across the world.

### Logo Construction
The logo is built on a **grid system**. The square is the smallest unit and is used to scale the logo with the correct proportions, respecting the arrangement of all its elements.

- Logo font: **Best Wishes** — a script/handwritten typeface
  - Download: https://www.dafont.com/es/best-wishes-2.font
  - Character: flexible, easy to read, manuscript style
  - Color combination highlights the word "Bunn" — *Café en TBD*, a major coffee-exporting region

## 2. Logo Versions

### Official Color Version
The official corporate logo has **deep black as the dominant base**. It is designed to always appear on dark substrates — specifically the deep black (`#262626`) described in the color palette.

### Black & White / Single Ink
Two single-ink variants are provided:
- **White version** — logo on white background
- **Black version** — logo on pure black (`#000000`) background

These are used to optimize ink usage and play with the color of the base material.

Together, these colors remind us that coffee is not just a drink, but a ritual connecting us to the intensity of life and the calm of contemplation.

## 4. Typography

### Logo Typeface — Best Wishes
Used exclusively for the logotype. Script/handwritten style, flexible and easy to read.
- Download: https://www.dafont.com/best-wishes-2.font
- Use: The handwritten font may be used when writing words in English in digital pieces.

### Corporate Typeface — Raleway
Raleway is the primary corporate typeface for all brand communications.
- Download: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Raleway

**Key benefits:**
- **Legibility** — excellent readability on screen and in print; ideal for communicating information effectively
- **Modern style** — clean, contemporary design gives projects a current, professional appearance
- **Weight & style variety** — range of weights and styles enables visual hierarchy and aesthetic flexibility
- **Versatility** — suitable for websites, printed documents, and presentations
- **Compatibility** — widely compatible with graphic design platforms and programs
- **Mobile-friendly** — legibility on mobile devices makes it an ideal choice for responsive design

**Additional typefaces loaded in brand assets (supporting use):**
- Inter
- Actor
- Roboto
- Roboto Condensed

## 5. Logo Usage — Incorrect Applications

The following uses are **strictly prohibited**:

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| No background color changes | Do not change the background color — always maintain colors from the official palette |
| No intermediate element color changes | Do not change the color of intermediate elements within the logo |
| No total or partial color alterations | Do not alter the logo colors in any way, fully or partially |
| No distortion | Do not deform the width or height of the logo — always maintain correct proportions |
| No element separation | Do not separate the elements of the logo composition, unless using only the symbol on its own |
| No rotation | Do not tilt the logo — always use it horizontal at 90 degrees |
| No obstructing elements | Do not place elements behind the logo that interfere with its correct visualization |
| No added borders or shadows | Do not add additional borders or shadows to the logo |

## 6. Photography Guidelines

The most important aspects to highlight in photography relate to **knowledge and delight**. Photography must demonstrate that Coffee Bunn Café is a brand that knows its product deeply and the processes involved in creating the best coffee.

### Key principles:
- **Authenticity** — people in images must not be posing; they should be in real situations, casual moments where a person can enjoy and savor a good coffee
- **Knowledge** — imagery should convey expertise in coffee culture and production processes
- **Graphic intervention** — graphic treatment of each photograph is essential to position the brand, with or without the logo present
- **Filters and tonal elements** — photos should include brand-consistent elements such as color filters, tonalities, and graphic elements (e.g., coffee leaf textures)

## 10. Digital Applications (Digitales)

### Brand Aesthetic for Digital
Digital brand presence follows a vivid, energetic look — mixing the vibrant yellow against the elegance of black. Coffee is the central communication element; coffee leaves accompany elements in each piece.

| Platform | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **PowerPoint (PPT)** | Full brand aesthetic integration using photography and the complete chromatic range |
| **Email signature (Firma de Correo electrónico)** | Black base highlights the logo, accompanied by coffee leaf texture |
| **Facebook (Cover, profile image, post)** | 3 pieces with key brand elements: color filters on photos, corporate fonts (Best Wishes + Raleway), Spanglish, coffee leaves, people consuming the product; yellow and black tones |
| **YouTube (Cover — multi-format)** | Official YouTube channel visual with adaptations for mobile and TV formats to show more product information |
| **Profile photos (Fotos de perfil)** | Yellow or deep black backgrounds using corporate colors; at this small size, no additional brand elements are placed |
| **Instagram (Post, Feed, IG TV)** | Very vibrant look; heavy use of yellow's vibrancy supported by black's elegance; coffee as the central element; coffee leaves accompany all pieces; handwritten font used for English-language words |

### Digital Design Principles
- Color filters on all photography consistent with brand palette
- Fonts: Best Wishes (for English/script uses) + Raleway (body and headings)
- "Spanglish" (Spanish/English mix) is a signature brand tone in digital copy
- Coffee leaf texture as a recurring graphic element
- Symbol can appear independently of the full logo in digital pieces
- Avoid logo saturation — not every piece needs the full logotype

## 12. Brand Voice & Tone

While not stated as an explicit section in the brand manual, the following principles are evident throughout all applications:

- **Community-centered** — the audience is called "Coffee Lovers" (`#CoffeeLovers`); this hashtag and identity label is used consistently
- **Knowledgeable** — the brand positions itself as a mentor and expert in coffee culture, capable of teaching others
- **Warm and approachable** — casual, real moments; no staged posing
- **Playful bilingualism** — "Spanglish" (Spanish + English mix) is a deliberate tonal choice used across packaging, napkins, cups, and digital pieces to create a distinctive, modern voice
- **Minimalist restraint** — the brand avoids logo saturation; not every touchpoint needs the full logo; the symbol and texture can carry identity on their own
- **Hashtags in use:** `#CoffeeBunnCafé`, `#CoffeeLovers`

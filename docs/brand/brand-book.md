# PART I — BRAND FOUNDATION

## Why We Exist

Most corporate gifts are forgotten the day after they're received.

A bottle of wine. A branded pen. A gift card in an envelope.

They're not bad gifts — they're just not *chosen*. They're a budget line item. A transaction dressed as appreciation.

CBC exists because we believe that when a company takes the time to give something meaningful — something with a story, with a face, with expertise behind it — that gift changes how the recipient feels about the person who gave it.

Coffee is our medium. Curation is our method. The connection it creates is our purpose.

---

## Brand Purpose

**To make the act of corporate gifting feel personal again.**

---

## Brand Mission

To deliver specialty coffee gift boxes that carry the expertise of Lorena Luna into every company that orders — so that each recipient not only receives a great coffee, but understands why it's great.

---

## Brand Vision

To become the reference for specialty coffee corporate gifting in Mexico — the brand that companies choose when they want the gift to reflect their own standards.

---

## Brand Values

### 1. Experticia — *Expertise that shows, not expertise that tells*
We don't say we know coffee. The Curation Card proves it. The micro-lot selection proves it. The class proves it. CBC never talks up its knowledge — it demonstrates it through specificity.

*In practice: we name the finca, the variety, the process, the tasting notes. We don't say "delicious artisanal coffee." We say "Geisha from Finca Los Altos, Veracruz, natural process, notes of white peach and jasmine."*

### 2. Autenticidad — *Honest about what this is*
The coffee changes because the best coffee changes. The price is what it is because quality has a cost. The branding is included because it should be. CBC doesn't hide behind marketing language — it says exactly what it is and what you get.

*In practice: the copy is direct, not flowery. We don't oversell. We describe accurately.*

### 3. Detalle — *The details are the product*
The Curation Card. The co-branded box. The class at 15 boxes. These are not add-ons. They are the thing that makes the gift worth giving. CBC believes that the quality of a gift is in the details the recipient didn't expect.

*In practice: every customer touchpoint — the box, the card, the WhatsApp reply, the invoice — reflects the same level of care.*

### 4. Conexión — *Coffee as a connector*
Coffee is one of the few things people make together, give to each other, and share a ritual around. CBC gifts are not objects — they are an invitation to a shared moment. The class is the purest expression of this: the whole team, the same cup, the same story.

*In practice: content, copy, and product always point toward the human moment — the team in a meeting, the client who gets the box, the person who reads the Curation Card.*

### 5. Excelencia sin pretensión — *Premium without being precious*
CBC is specialty coffee. It is not trying to be accessible to everyone. But it is also not exclusive, cold, or intimidating. Lorena talks to people the way a knowledgeable friend talks — with warmth, in plain language, without making you feel like you don't belong.

*In practice: no industry jargon without explanation. No condescension. No corporate stiffness. But no dumbing down either.*

# PART II — BRAND IDENTITY

## The Name

**Coffee Bunn Café** — abbreviated **CBC**.

- "Bunn" is a deliberate invention — it references the coffee bean ("bun," "bean") without being literal. It gives the brand a distinctive, ownable name that doesn't translate or get lost.
- "Café" at the end anchors it in Spanish and in the product category.
- The full name is used in formal contexts. CBC is used in operational and digital contexts.
- The hashtag is **#CoffeeBunnCafe** (no accent in the hashtag).

**Never abbreviate to just "Coffee" or "Café." Never write "Coffee Bunn" without "Café" in formal usage.**

## The Symbol

The **coffee plant (mata de café)** is CBC's symbol. It can be used independently of the full logotype — on cups, as a stamp, as a watermark — without the full name.

The symbol represents: care, cultivation, patience, and the craft behind every cup. It is not decorative. It carries meaning.

## Logo Usage Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Preferred background | Deep Black (#262626) — the logo lives on dark substrates |
| Single-ink white version | Use on white packaging or documents |
| Single-ink black version | Use on pure black (#000000) substrate |
| Never distort | Maintain proportions — scale by the grid unit |
| Never rotate | Horizontal only, 90 degrees |
| Never separate elements | Logo + symbol stay together unless using symbol alone |
| Never add effects | No drop shadows, no borders, no outlines |

| Never on busy backgrounds | Logo needs clear visual space |

# PART III — VOICE & TONE

## The Voice of CBC

CBC has one voice. It sounds like Lorena talking to someone she respects — a peer, not a prospect.

**Four words that describe the CBC voice:**
1. **Clara** — says what it means, no filler
2. **Cálida** — personal, uses "tú", never institutional
3. **Precisa** — names things accurately, never vague
4. **Natural** — moves between Spanish and English without announcing it

## Tone by Context

The voice stays constant. The tone adjusts to the room.

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Instagram / Facebook | Warm, personal, a little storytelling | "Este café viene de una finca en Oaxaca que cultiva en sombra. El primer sorbo huele a durazno." |
| LinkedIn | Professional but human, thought leadership | "Most corporate gifts are forgotten by Friday. Here's what makes a coffee gift different." |
| WhatsApp (sales) | Direct, helpful, zero pressure | "Te mando la cotización en un momento. ¿Cuántas cajas serían y para cuándo las necesitas?" |
| Curation Card | Poetic, specific, intimate — Lorena to one person | "Elegí este café porque la cosecha de este año en Finca Los Altos fue excepcional. Lo vas a notar desde el primer aroma." |
| Customer service | Fast, empathetic, solution-first | "Entiendo, revisamos el pedido ahorita y te digo qué pasó." |
| Quote / invoice | Professional, clear, warm close | "Quedo pendiente de cualquier duda. Gracias por confiar en CBC." |

---

## Writing Principles

### 1. Name the thing
Never say "delicioso café de especialidad." Say "Geisha de Veracruz, proceso natural, notas de durazno y flor de naranja."
Specificity is credibility. Generality is forgettable.

### 2. First line stops the scroll
Every post, every email, every message starts with a line that earns the next one.
Bad: *"En Coffee Bunn Café nos especializamos en..."*
Good: *"Este café lo eligió Lorena el martes. El viernes va en tus cajas."*

### 3. Short is almost always right
If you can say it in 3 lines, don't use 5. CBC copy is edited, not padded.

### 4. The CTA is one thing
Every piece of content ends with one action. Not three. Not "follow us, share this, and visit our website." One.

### 5. Spanglish is natural, not a device
CBC naturally mixes Spanish and English — especially on packaging and in social content. It is not a brand trick. It is how Lorena talks. It signals a bicultural, modern sensibility.
*"Dame un Smile al beber tu taza de café."*
*"For the Coffee Lovers que saben lo que quieren."*

### 6. Never apologize for being premium
CBC does not discount in copy. Does not say "asequible" or "para todos los presupuestos." The price is the price. The quality is the quality. Confidence is the correct register.

## The Lorena Voice

When writing as Lorena (first person, Instagram Reels, Curation Cards, LinkedIn posts), the voice becomes even more personal.

**Lorena's first-person voice:**
- Speaks with quiet authority — she knows this, she chose this, she's telling you why
- Uses "este café" not "nuestro café" — she's showing you something, not selling
- Makes coffee specific and human simultaneously
- Occasionally expresses genuine excitement — but earns it with detail first

**Example (wrong):**
*"Hola a todos! Hoy quiero compartir con ustedes el café que seleccioné esta semana. Es un café increíble de México que estoy segura que van a amar. 💕"*

**Example (right):**
*"Este micro-lote llegó de Chiapas la semana pasada. Cuando abrí el saco, el aroma era suficiente: floral, ligeramente frutal, sin acidez agresiva. Proceso honey. Variedad Bourbon. Va a estar en las cajas de este mes — y es uno de los mejores que he seleccionado en el año."*

# PART IV — MESSAGING FRAMEWORK

## Core Message (Brand Promise)

> *"El mejor café disponible, en manos de quien lo va a apreciar — con la historia incluida."*

Everything CBC does is in service of this promise.

## Messaging by Audience Segment

### HR Managers / People & Culture
**Their job:** Make employees feel valued. Deliver something people actually like.
**Their fear:** Choosing something that flops. Looking like they didn't put thought into it.
**CBC message:** *"El regalo que tus colaboradores van a recordar — y que no te va a hacer quedar mal."*
**Proof points:** Curation Card (shows thought), Lorena's expertise (legitimizes), co-branding (makes it look intentional), the class (makes it an experience, not just an object)

### Marketing / Communications Teams
**Their job:** Reinforce brand values through every touchpoint, including gifting.
**Their fear:** Gifts that feel off-brand or generic.
**CBC message:** *"Un regalo con identidad propia que complementa la tuya."*
**Proof points:** Co-branded packaging (their logo + CBC = quality association), the micro-lot story (narrative content for internal comms), brand manual alignment

### Procurement / Office Managers
**Their job:** Deliver on spec, on time, within budget.
**Their fear:** Supplier reliability, surprises, complaints.
**CBC message:** *"Calidad verificable, entrega puntual, branding incluido — sin sorpresas."*
**Proof points:** Clear MOQ (10 boxes), defined lead time (7–10 days), fixed price ($799), order tracking

### C-Suite / Executive Gifting (consultative tier)
**Their need:** The gift needs to signal taste, not just generosity. It's a relationship gesture.
**Their expectation:** Someone who understands what they're doing without needing to explain it.
**CBC message:** *"Para quienes aprecian que alguien pensó en esto."*
**Proof points:** Lorena's personal involvement, Kyoto brew and above, bespoke curation, no catalog — direct conversation

## Product Messaging

### The Coffee
- **What to say:** "El mejor micro-lote disponible en el momento de tu pedido — seleccionado por Lorena."
- **What not to say:** "Café de alta calidad" / "café premium" / "café 100% mexicano" (the origin varies)
- **The insight to convey:** The coffee changes = it's always the best available = it's never stale or arbitrary

### The Curation Card
- **What to say:** "Una tarjeta escrita por Lorena con la historia de este café específico."
- **What not to say:** "Una guía de preparación incluida"
- **The insight:** It's not instructions. It's Lorena talking directly to the recipient. That's the differentiator.

### The Co-Branding
- **What to say:** "El logo de tu empresa va en la caja — incluido, sin costo adicional."
- **What not to say:** "Opción de personalización disponible"
- **The insight:** It's not an option. It's standard. That's how CBC works.

### The 15-Box Class
- **What to say:** "Con 15 cajas o más, Lorena imparte una clase en vivo para todo tu equipo."
- **What not to say:** "Bonus gratuito para pedidos grandes"
- **The insight:** It converts a one-time gift into a shared team experience. Frame it as the thing that makes this worth getting to 15.

# PART VI — BRAND GUARDRAILS

## What CBC Is
- A specialty coffee **corporate gifting** brand
- A **B2B** business — the primary customer is a company, not an individual
- A **curated** brand — Lorena's judgment is the product
- A **premium** offering — $799+ per box is the floor, not the ceiling
- A **Mexican** brand (based in CDMX, coffee from Mexico and beyond)

## What CBC Is Not
- A café or physical restaurant
- A coffee subscription service
- A DTC / consumer brand
- A "Colombian" brand (Lorena is Colombian; CBC is not)
- A discounting brand
- A generic gifting company that happens to carry coffee

## The Three Things That Must Always Be True

1. **The coffee has a story** — every box includes specific information about what's inside and why it was chosen. If this disappears, CBC is just another coffee brand.

2. **Lorena's expertise is present** — whether through the Curation Card, the class, or the consultative tier, Lorena's knowledge is the reason CBC exists. If she becomes invisible, CBC loses its reason to exist.

3. **The gift feels intentional** — the branding, the card, the class incentive. Everything communicates that someone thought about this. The moment it looks generic, it's over.

# PART I — STRATEGY

## The One Job of CBC Marketing

**Get the right person to start a WhatsApp conversation.**

Not to go viral. Not to get followers. Not to build awareness broadly.

Marketing's job is to generate qualified leads — HR managers, marketing coordinators, and office managers at CDMX companies who are looking for a corporate gift and need one reason to reach out.

Every channel, every post, every piece of content exists to serve that one job.

---

## The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary Target — The Corporate Gift Decision-Maker

**Who:**
- Job title: HR Manager, People & Culture Lead, Office Manager, Marketing Coordinator, Executive Assistant
- Company size: 20–500 employees
- Location: CDMX (primary), Monterrey and Guadalajara (secondary)
- Industries (highest gifting budgets, in order): Technology, Financial services, Legal, Real estate, Management consulting, Pharmaceutical, Architecture & design

**What they need:**
- A gift that doesn't embarrass them in front of their boss
- Something that can be explained and justified
- Supplier reliability (MOQ clear, lead time clear, delivery on time)
- A story they can tell internally ("we gifted specialty coffee curated by a barista expert, with their team's logo on it")

**What they fear:**
- Choosing something generic that gets forgotten
- A supplier that doesn't deliver on time
- Budget approval being questioned

**How they decide:**
- Instagram or LinkedIn discovery → they look at the profile
- WhatsApp conversation → quote within 24 hours
- Decision within 3–7 days
- Often one person decides; sometimes needs manager approval for large orders

**What makes them say yes fast:**
- They can see what the product looks like (photography)
- The price is clear and feels justified
- The co-branding is standard (not an add-on)
- Someone responds to their WhatsApp within 2 hours

**CBC's job at each stage:**

## Instagram — Channel Playbook

### Role
Instagram is where corporate buyers verify that CBC is real, beautiful, and trustworthy. They don't come to Instagram to buy — they come to confirm that the decision they're considering is a good one.

### Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: El Café** (40% of content)
The current micro-lot. Its story, its origin, what it tastes like, why Lorena chose it.
This is the most important pillar — it is the daily proof that CBC's expertise is real.

*Formats: Reels (Lorena talking about the coffee), carousels (origin info + tasting notes), close-up photography*

**Pillar 2: El Regalo** (30% of content)
The gift box as a product. The unboxing. The Curation Card. The co-branded box. The accessory.
This is the pillar that converts consideration into desire.

*Formats: Product photography, unboxing content, co-branding reveal, before/after (plain box vs. branded)*

**Pillar 3: La Experiencia** (20% of content)
The team. The class. The moment someone opens the box. The human context around the gift.
This is the emotional pillar — it shows what the gift *does*, not just what it is.

*Formats: Lifestyle photography, class screenshots (with permission), reactions, company logos as social proof*

**Pillar 4: El Conocimiento** (10% of content)
Coffee education, interesting facts, industry news, the vocabulary of specialty coffee.
This is the authority pillar — it positions CBC and Lorena as the experts in the room.

*Formats: Carousels (educational), quote cards, short Reels*

### Posting Schedule

| Day | Content Type | Pillar |
|-----|-------------|--------|

| Wednesday | Coffee Reel or carousel — Lorena explains the micro-lot | El Café |
| Friday | (Optional) Social proof, class recap, or knowledge card | La Experiencia / El Conocimiento |

## LinkedIn — Channel Playbook

### Role
LinkedIn is where Lorena's personal brand operates as the primary trust signal for corporate buyers. It is the highest-quality B2B discovery channel in Mexico.

LinkedIn does not replace Instagram — it targets a different moment. An HR manager who sees CBC on Instagram might also check LinkedIn before deciding. A procurement manager might only discover CBC through LinkedIn.

### The Strategy: Lorena, Not CBC
The LinkedIn presence is Lorena's **personal profile** — not a company page.

Corporate buyers trust people more than brands. A person with genuine expertise posting about specialty coffee and corporate gifting is more compelling than a brand page posting the same thing.

**Lorena's LinkedIn profile must communicate:**
- She is the specialty coffee expert behind CBC
- She has deep knowledge of the full production chain
- CBC is her project — the gift boxes are an expression of her expertise
- She is available for corporate gifting conversations

### Post Types (rotate)

**Type 1: The Micro-Lot Story**
Lorena announces the coffee she chose this week. What it is, where it's from, why she selected it. Brings her expertise to life in a specific, repeatable format.

**Type 2: The Corporate Gift Insight**
An observation about corporate gifting — why most gifts fail, what makes a gift memorable, why the story matters. Positions Lorena as a thought leader in the gifting space, not just coffee.

**Type 3: The Class Recap**
After hosting a class for a corporate client, Lorena posts about the experience (with client permission). The most powerful social proof — shows the product working.

**Type 4: Coffee Education**
A short piece of education about specialty coffee — what "micro-lot" means, how natural vs. washed processing affects flavor, what to look for in a coffee tasting. Builds authority, earns follows from the right people.

### LinkedIn Caption Formula

```
[BOLD OPENING STATEMENT — standalone sentence, earns engagement]

[EXPANDING PARAGRAPH — 3–5 sentences, makes the statement real]

[SPECIFIC EXAMPLE OR STORY — grounds it in something concrete]

[INSIGHT OR TAKEAWAY — what should the reader think differently about now]

[SOFT CTA — question, DM invitation, or link]
```

```

## WhatsApp — Channel Playbook

### Role
WhatsApp is where sales happen. Period. Every other channel exists to generate a WhatsApp conversation. This is the close channel.

### Response Rules
- **Response time target:** Under 2 hours, business days
- **Tone:** Personal, direct, helpful — never scripted-sounding
- **First response:** Always acknowledge + ask the one question that unlocks the conversation

### Conversation Flow

**Inbound lead (from landing page or any channel):**
```
[ACKNOWLEDGE + QUALIFY]
"Hola [Nombre], gracias por escribir a Coffee Bunn Café.
Con gusto te cotizamos. ¿Para cuántas cajas estarías considerando
y para cuándo las necesitarías?"

[SEND CATALOG]
[Attach catalog PDF after they confirm quantity]

[QUOTE]
"Aquí va la cotización: [details]
Tiempo de producción: 7–10 días hábiles con branding.
Para confirmar, necesitamos el 50% de anticipo y tu logo en alta.

[CLOSE]

# PART III — CONTENT SYSTEM

## The Content Engine (Automated)

CBC uses an autonomous content engine that posts to Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn without manual intervention. It runs on Railway and uses:
- **Claude** to generate captions and image prompts
- **DALL-E 3** to generate brand-style images
- **Meta Graph API** to publish to Instagram and Facebook
- **LinkedIn API** to publish to LinkedIn

Lorena's only interaction: send a WhatsApp message to update the current coffee when she changes the micro-lot. Everything else is automatic.

### Automated Schedule

| Day | Platform | Content Type | Pillar |
|-----|----------|-------------|--------|
| Monday | IG + FB | Product post | El Regalo |
| Wednesday | IG + FB | Coffee story | El Café |
| 1st of month | LinkedIn | Lorena post | Thought leadership |
| 15th of month | LinkedIn | Lorena post | Thought leadership |
| Daily (if season active) | All | Seasonal campaign | Seasonal |

### Coffee Update Flow (Lorena's input)

When Lorena selects a new micro-lot, she sends one WhatsApp message to the CBC business number:

**Format (free text — Claude parses it):**
```
Café nuevo: [Name]. Origen: [Region, Country], Finca [Farm name].
Variedad [Variety]. Proceso [process]. Notas de cata: [note 1, note 2, note 3].
[Optional: any story or context she wants used]
```

**System response:** Confirmation with parsed data. Next content cycle uses the new coffee.

# PART V — CAMPAIGN MECHANICS

## How to Build a Seasonal Campaign

### 6 weeks before peak:

**Week 1 — Announce**
- 1 Instagram post: "La temporada de [Fin de año / Amor / Trabajo] se acerca. Estas cajas tienen historia."
- 1 LinkedIn post: Why specialty coffee is the corporate gift this season
- WhatsApp broadcast to past clients: "Hola, [Nombre] — se acerca [season], ¿ya están pensando en los regalos de su equipo?"

**Week 2 — Educate**
- 1 Instagram carousel: "¿Qué hace especial una caja de café CBC?"
- 1 Coffee story post: the current micro-lot and why it's perfect for gifting

**Week 3 — Social Proof**
- Post a client testimonial or order reveal (if available)
- LinkedIn: class recap or client story

**Week 4 — Urgency (first)**
- "Últimos días para personalización con branding. Los pedidos con logo cierran el [date]."
- WhatsApp outreach to warm leads that didn't close

**Week 5 — Urgency (final)**
- "Cierre de pedidos [date]. Lo que llegue después no lleva branding personalizado."
- LinkedIn: "If you're still deciding on corporate gifts, here's what to know."

**Week 6 — Close / Thank you**
- "Cerramos pedidos de temporada. Gracias a las empresas que confiaron en CBC."
- Begin collecting testimonials from delivered orders

## What to Iterate On

**If leads aren't coming:**
- Problem is in Discovery or Consideration
- Fix: more LinkedIn outreach, better photography, update landing page hero

**If leads come but don't convert:**
- Problem is in Decision
- Fix: faster response time, clearer quote, better catalog PDF

**If orders are small (under 15 boxes):**
- Fix: emphasize the 15-box class in every conversation and post

**If no repeat customers:**
- Fix: improve seasonal outreach to past clients, start referral program

**If content feels generic:**
- Return to the Brand Book. Check the voice. Check the pillar balance.
- Run more Pillar 1 (El Café) — it's the hardest to fake and the most trusted.
